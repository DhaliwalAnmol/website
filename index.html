Decemember 2020

FRC 2018 Power Up CAD: Drivetrain

The drivetrain is a six wheel tank drive with an H frame. The drivetrain is constructed using sheetmetal. The H frame is to allow the intake to bring cubes into the robot without having to raise the cube over the rail. That decreases intake time and complexity of the intake sequence. This drivetrain also features the S rail design to simplify mounting solutions. The drivetrain sports the 2 Cim Ball Shifter gearbox from Vex Pro. The drivetrain also features tensioning block to make sure that chain stays tight at all times. The high gear speed of the robot is 22 feet per second and the low gear speed is 15 feet per second. The total dimension of the drivetrain are 27.5 inches wide by 32 inches long.

Program/Team : Solidoworks, FRC

Decemeber 2020

FRC 2018 Power Up CAD: Elevator

The elevator is a one stage plus carriage cascading elevator with 94.75 inches of travel. The carriage is still in development. The elevator features an interesting way of rigging. Previously rigging was done outside in open space in the robot. This leaves the rigging exposed to being broken by other robots. This elevator has the rigging inside the tubes to make sure its protected. On previous FRC elevators made by the team ball bearings on the inside of a stage would increase the space between stages by a considerable amount. The ball bearings are now mounted on inserts on the ends of the tube instead of outside of the tube. This decreases space between stages allowing for more room in the centre of the robot. The elevator also doubles as a climber. The gearbox has two speeds. One is for regular elevator movement and the second speed is for climbing. The elevator has a full extension time of 1.73 seconds under load and an average travel speed of 55.2 inches per second. The gearbox has a 693 pound stall load which ensures the elevator will never encounter stall.

Program/Team : Solidworks, FRC

VRC 2020 Change Up Drivetrain

The drivetrain was made for the 2020 VRC game Changeup. This drivetrain was able to be built due to Covid-19 cancelling the competition. The 6 wheel omni drive features locked omni wheels in the centre. This is to minimize the sideways pushing from other robots. The drivetrain is powered by 4 Vex V5 motors. The gear ratio on the drivetrain is 0.6 to 1. This ensures that we have enough torque to push other robots while also maintaining a high speed. The major dimensions of the robot are 15 inches wide and 15 inches long.

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge Robot

The FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge robot is FRC Team 1325's 2020 competition robot. It is a 125 pound industrial grade robot. Created the climber and drivetrain subsystem. The robot was manufactured and used in competition until season suspension.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Drivetrain

The drivetrain is six-wheel tank drive and sheet metal construction has some key improvements and changes from previous year designs. The foremost of which is the S rail, which allows for easier mounting to the side rails of the drivetrain. The drivetrain has 1.50” of ground clearance with a 0.125” drop centre to allow it to scale the berm in the middle of the field. The Drivetrain is 26” wide and 32” long. It also features adjustable chain tensioning mounting for gearboxes and wheels. There is enough space to configure the wheels to 1.5” wide traction wheels instead of 1” wide, in case it encounters heavy defence from opposing robots. The drivetrain features the 2 Cim Ball Shifter gearbox and has a low gear speed of 9.25 feet per second and a high gear speed of 21 feet per second.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Drivetrain S Rail

The inside side rail of the drivetrain creates an S shape, instead of a C shape like past years. Allows for direct mounting of tubing and other parts to the side of the drivetrain. In previous years spacers were used to mount to the side of the drivetrain. The spacers caused difficulty to mount and made service harder. Built in chain tensioners so that chain runs are tight and always perform at their best. Chain fatigues over the course of the competitions and becomes looser, chain tensioning combats that problem. It allows for chain to tightened easily without having to replace the chain run.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Climber

The climber design is centred around a one-stage cascading elevator and the custom bearing blocks designed for it. This climber has a total travel of 76”, 400 - pound stall load and travel time under load of 1.5 seconds. This climber fits 76 inches of travel into just a two-stage elevator, this was done to save weight from bearing block and additional aluminum extrusion while also being a simpler solution to ensure easier maintenance over the course of the season. The elevator is supported by a set of 1/16 wall round tubes on rod-ends, this allows for a lightweight and unobtrusive superstructure that is just as resilient as other moderate superstructure designs. The superstructure is mounted on the turret and shooter to counteract the moment created by the shooter and turret. The elevator is powered by 2 custom gearboxes featuring a pneumatic actuated winch and pawl break.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Climber Bearing Blocks

The climber utilizes new bearing block designs that have not been used on elevators by the team in previous years. In the past, ball bearings were used as a sliding surface for the elevator. This elevator features HTPE sliding blocks as a sliding surface. This allowed for a lightweight and thinner design. Since the climber would only raise and lower once a match it was feasible to use a simpler and less robust solution than ball bearings.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Climber Hooks

The hooks are machined our of 3/8 aluminum. They are design to hook onto a circular tube. The hook has tapped holes for mounting which makes it faster to service, repair and mount the hooks.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Climber Gearbox

There are 2 custom gearboxes on the climber each sporting one 775pro on a 17:1 overall reduction resulting in 400 pounds of load until stall and a loaded travel speed of about 50 inches per second. The 400 pounds of stall load allows for the climb to never be affected by stall since it will only lift a maximum of 156 pounds.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

January 2020

FRC 2020 Infinite Recharge: Climber Gearbox Brake

In competition climbs are scored 5 seconds after the match is over if the robot is not touching the ground. Which means the climber needs keep the robot hanging without power. The brake features a pneumatic actuated winch and pawl system. The brake is place strategically in the gear reduction to minimize load. The calculated load on the brake is 10 N. The brake is featured in each gearbox which results in the climber having 2 brakes in total.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

FRC 2019 Offseason Demonstration Robot

The 2019 offseason Demo Robot was an offseason project for FRC Team 1325. The team struggled with transportation of a 156 pound robot. The purpose of the Demo Robot was to make a small 2 foot by 2 foot by 2 foot robot that maintains the industrial grade of an FRC robot, but fits in a SUV. November 2019

FRC 2019 Offseason Demonstration Robot: Intake

The intake for this robot is designed to pick up the 2017 FRC balls. The intake is constructed out of 1/4 inch aluminum plates, 2 rollers and a polycarbonate ramp. The intake is powered by a 775 Pro motor on a 3:1 belt ratio. The motor is mounted on a swivel to ensure that to make sure that we can tighten the belt without replacing it. The rollers are belted together. To ensure that those belts remain tight they are tensioned by a ball bearing pushing into them on a slot.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC

November 2019

FRC 2019 Offseason Demonstration Robot: Hopper

The hopper is constructed our of 1/16 polycarbonate. The job of the hopper is to hold intake balls while the indexer feeds balls into the shooter. The total capacity of the hopper is about 25 balls. There are 2 configurations to the hopper travel and storage. In the travel configuration the hopper stays in the frame perimeter and maintains a small footprint for easy transportation. In storage mode the hopper extends over the intake to hold balls. Since the hopper extends over the intake it ensures that the robot never loses a ball once the robot intakes.

Program/Team : Solidworks , FRC
